Phia Creative Brief — Week of May 19
3 angles to test, grounded in competitor gaps and what the feed is not yet saying
Angle 01 — Gap Play
"Your savings app for fashion. Not debt."
Klarna/BNPL is the only "savings" product running on Meta — and it sells debt. Phia is the opposite. Target fashion-conscious women 22–34 who have used BNPL. They owe money; you earned cashback.
CTA: "See how much you're leaving on the table"
Angle 02 — Luxury Access
"Shop Miu Miu. Don't pay Miu Miu prices."
Vestiaire + Poshmark push Miu Miu / Hermes / Gucci — but they sell used. Phia lets you buy new, from the real brand, with cashback. Same luxury aspiration, smarter price.
CTA: "Find your new bag — and get money back"
Angle 03 — Community Trust
"She told you before you bought it."
Nobody is running the "trusted friend who knows where to shop" angle. Phia is a curation layer. UGC-first: real Phia users sharing what they found and what they saved.
CTA: "What she used to save $43 on her last order"

Key Gaps Competitors Are Leaving Open for Phia

Mercari + Grailed are dark on Meta — their fashion-resale audience is sitting unaddressed on IG right now.
The entire savings-app category (Honey, Rakuten, Capital One Shopping) has zero Meta social presence — Phia can claim this identity exclusively.
No competitor combines luxury brand names + cashback/savings in a single creative. This is Phia's natural intersection and nobody occupies it.
ThredUp + Poshmark run separate buyer/seller campaigns — Phia can run a unified "shop smarter" message serving both intents.
Sustainability language is fully owned by ThredUp — avoid it this week. Lean into financial intelligence + style identity instead.
